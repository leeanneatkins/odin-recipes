Vegetarian Chili

Description

This vegetarian chili is perfect for a cold winter day. The secret ingredient is cocoa powder. It adds a nice, smoky depth that enhances the other flavors. I always serve this with hot cornbread with lots of melted honey butter. I found this recipe at https://www.noracooks.com/ultimate-vegan-chili/

Ingredients

  • 14 oz package Gardein plant-based beefless crumbles (or other meat substitute)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium sweet onion, diced
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • (2) 28-oz cans crushed tomatoes
  • (2) 15-oz cans black beans, drained and rinsed
  • (1) 15-oz can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon pure maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, add the olive oil.
  2. Add the chopped onion and saut√© 3-4 minutes until translucent.
  3. Add in the garlic and cook 1 more minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Now add all the rest of the chili ingredients, except the meatless crumbles, and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes.
  5. Stir the meatless crumbles into the pot and heat a few minutes until the are warm. (If a thicker consistency is desired, use an immersion blender and blend just a few times. Do this before you add the meatless crumbles.)
  6. Serve with sour cream, cornbread, tortilla chips, or cilantro, if desired.
